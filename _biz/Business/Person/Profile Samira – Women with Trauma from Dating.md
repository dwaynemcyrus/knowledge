## Norms to Change
[[Enemy to Fight|New ways of being]] to get Samira to see and desire the value in for her life.

For women experiencing trauma from dating, certain accepted norms or beliefs may need to change to benefit their wellbeing and transformation. Here’s a list of norms that could be addressed to facilitate healing and personal growth:

1. Self-Blame for Failed Relationships
	- Old Norm: Believing that relationship failures are solely their fault.
	- New Norm: Understanding that relationship issues are often complex and not a reflection of their worth.
2. Settling for Less
	- Old Norm: Accepting poor treatment or subpar relationships out of fear of being alone.
	- New Norm: Valuing themselves enough to seek and maintain healthy, respectful relationships.
3. Fear of Rejection
	- Old Norm: Avoiding new relationships or opportunities out of fear of being rejected.
	- New Norm: Embracing the possibility of rejection as part of the growth process and focusing on self-worth.
4. Negative Self-Perception
	- Old Norm: Holding a negative self-image based on past relationship experiences.
	- New Norm: Cultivating a positive self-image and recognizing their inherent value independent of relationship status.
5. Suppressing Needs and Desires
	- Old Norm: Not expressing their needs and desires to avoid conflict or being seen as demanding.
	- New Norm: Clearly communicating their needs and desires and expecting them to be respected.
6. Avoidance of Emotional Vulnerability
	- Old Norm: Keeping emotions hidden to avoid being hurt again.
	- New Norm: Allowing themselves to be emotionally vulnerable in safe, supportive environments.
7. Distrust in Potential Partners
	- Old Norm: Assuming that all potential partners will eventually cause harm.
	- New Norm: Learning to assess partners individually and building trust slowly and carefully.
8. Over-Reliance on External Validation
	- Old Norm: Seeking validation and worth from relationships and partners.
	- New Norm: Developing self-validation and self-esteem independent of external approval.
9. Fear of Being Alone
	- Old Norm: Entering or staying in relationships out of fear of loneliness.
	- New Norm: Embracing alone time as an opportunity for self-growth and understanding.
10.	Ignoring Red Flags
	- Old Norm: Overlooking early warning signs in relationships to avoid confrontation or loss.
	- New Norm: Recognizing and acting on red flags early to protect their wellbeing.
11.	Attachment to Unhealthy Patterns
	- Old Norm: Repeating negative relationship patterns due to familiarity.
	- New Norm: Breaking free from unhealthy patterns by seeking healthier relationship dynamics.
12.	Minimizing Their Own Trauma
	- Old Norm: Downplaying the impact of past relationship trauma.
	- New Norm: Acknowledging the significance of their experiences and seeking appropriate support.
13.	Belief in Changing Others
	- Old Norm: Believing they can change a partner’s negative behaviors through their own efforts.
	- New Norm: Accepting that they cannot change others and focusing on finding partners who are already aligned with their values and needs.
14.	Overlooking Self-Care
	- Old Norm: Prioritizing relationship needs over their own self-care.
	- New Norm: Making self-care a priority and understanding its importance in maintaining healthy relationships.
15.	Mistrust of Their Own Judgment
	- Old Norm: Doubting their ability to make good decisions in relationships.
	- New Norm: Building confidence in their judgment and decision-making skills through self-reflection and learning from past experiences.

By addressing and transforming these norms, women with trauma from dating can heal, regain their confidence, and build healthier, more fulfilling relationships.
