The Islamic (Hijri) calendar consists of 12 lunar months:
1. Muharram (المحرّم) – A sacred month; the first month of the Islamic year.
2. Safar (صفر) – Historically considered a month of hardship.
3. Rabi’ al-Awwal (ربيع الأول) – The month of the Prophet Muhammad’s birth.
4. Rabi’ al-Thani (ربيع الآخر) – Also known as Rabi’ al-Akhir.
5. Jumada al-Awwal (جمادى الأول) – A winter month in pre-Islamic times.
6. Jumada al-Thani (جمادى الآخر) – Also known as Jumada al-Akhir.
7. Rajab (رجب) – One of the four sacred months in Islam.
8. Sha’ban (شعبان) – The month before Ramadan, often for spiritual preparation.
9. Ramadan (رمضان) – The month of fasting, the holiest month.
10. Shawwal (شوّال) – The month after Ramadan, includes Eid al-Fitr.
11. Dhul-Qa’dah (ذو القعدة) – A sacred month, a time of truce in pre-Islamic times.
12. Dhul-Hijjah (ذو الحجة) – The month of Hajj and Eid al-Adha.

  

The Islamic calendar is a purely lunar calendar, making it about 10–12 days shorter than the Gregorian solar calendar.

- M03 Dhu al-Hijjah: (sacred) Possessor of the Pilgrimage" or "The Month of the Pilgrimage". During this month, Muslim pilgrims from all around the world congregate at Mecca to visit the Kaaba.

- M05 Safar: "journey" or "travel"

- M07 Rabi’ ath-thani: the second or last spring
- M08 Jumada al-ula: signals the beginning of the summer season. “Jamad” itself means “dry or rainless” / "to take charge with, commend, entrust, commit or care during the arid or cold month"

- M10 Rajab: (Sacred) classical Arabic verb rajaba is "**to respect**", which could also mean "be awe or be in fear", of which Rajab is a derivative. Rajab. / Planting seeds or ramadan
