## Boundary Journal 
### Day 6: Reflecting and Integrating Boundaries
**Assignment:** Create an ongoing boundary tracking journal.

**Purpose:** To encourage continuous reflection on boundary needs and violations, ultimately leading participants to seek further coaching support.

**Steps:**
1. **Set Up Your Journal:**
   - **Materials Needed:** A notebook or a few sheets of paper, and a pen.
   - **Sections:** Divide your journal into daily or weekly entries.

2. **Daily/Weekly Reflection:**
   - **Identify Boundary Needs:** Each day or week, write down any situations where you felt a boundary was needed. Be specific about the context and why you felt this way.
   - **Track Boundary Violations:** Record any instances where your boundaries were violated. Describe what happened, how it made you feel, and the impact on your well-being.
   - **Emotional Response:** Reflect on your emotional responses to these situations. Note any patterns or recurring feelings.

3. **Analyze Patterns:**
   - **Review Entries:** At the end of each week, review your entries to identify patterns or common themes in boundary needs and violations.
   - **Identify Areas for Improvement:** Note areas where you struggle to set or maintain boundaries and consider why these areas are challenging.

4. **Set Intentions:**
   - **Weekly Goals:** Set 1-3 goals for the upcoming week to improve your boundary-setting skills. These could include practicing assertive communication, reinforcing existing boundaries, or addressing specific situations.
   - **Action Steps:** Outline specific actions you will take to achieve these goals.

5. **Regular Check-Ins:**
   - **Schedule Time:** Dedicate time each week to review your journal and assess your progress. Adjust your goals and action steps as needed.

**Encouragement for Further Coaching:**
- **Reflect on Progress:** Use this ongoing exercise to gain insight into your boundary-setting journey. Recognize the areas where you’ve made progress and those that still need work.
- **Discovery Call Invitation:** If you find that you need more support in setting and maintaining boundaries, consider booking a discovery call to discuss how a 4 or 8-week coaching program can help you achieve your goals.

**Time Needed:** 10-15 minutes daily or 30-60 minutes weekly

This ongoing exercise will help participants continuously track and reflect on their boundary needs and experiences, encouraging them to seek additional support through coaching.

### 1. Deep Breathing Exercise
**Purpose:** Calm the mind and reduce stress through controlled breathing.

**Steps:**
1. **Find a comfortable position:** Sit or lie down in a comfortable position with your back straight.
2. **Close your eyes:** Gently close your eyes to minimize distractions.
3. **Inhale deeply:** Breathe in slowly through your nose for a count of four, allowing your abdomen to rise as your lungs fill with air.
4. **Hold your breath:** Hold the breath for a count of four.
5. **Exhale slowly:** Breathe out slowly through your mouth for a count of six, feeling the tension leaving your body.
6. **Repeat:** Continue this cycle for 5-10 minutes, focusing solely on your breath.

**Tip:** Place one hand on your chest and the other on your abdomen to feel the rise and fall of your breath, enhancing your focus.

### 2. Body Scan Meditation
**Purpose:** Increase body awareness and release tension by mentally scanning the body.

**Steps:**
1. **Get comfortable:** Lie down or sit comfortably with your eyes closed.
2. **Focus on your breath:** Take a few deep breaths to center yourself.
3. **Begin the scan:** Start at the top of your head and gradually move down through your body.
4. **Notice sensations:** Pay attention to any areas of tension, discomfort, or relaxation. Don’t try to change anything; just observe.
5. **Release tension:** As you notice areas of tension, imagine sending your breath to those areas, and as you exhale, imagine the tension melting away.
6. **Continue to the toes:** Move your focus slowly down to your toes, taking your time with each body part.
7. **Complete the scan:** Once you reach your toes, take a few deep breaths and gradually bring your awareness back to the present moment.

**Tip:** If your mind wanders, gently bring your focus back to the body part you were scanning.

### 3. Grounding Exercise
**Purpose:** Anchor yourself in the present moment to reduce anxiety and stress.

**Steps:**
1. **Stand or sit comfortably:** Ensure your feet are flat on the ground and your back is straight.
2. **Feel the ground:** Focus on the connection between your feet and the ground. Notice how the ground supports your weight.
3. **Engage your senses:**
   - **Sight:** Look around and identify five things you can see.
   - **Touch:** Identify four things you can touch and feel.
   - **Hearing:** Identify three things you can hear.
   - **Smell:** Identify two things you can smell.
   - **Taste:** Identify one thing you can taste.
4. **Deep breaths:** Take a few deep breaths, inhaling through your nose and exhaling through your mouth.
5. **Visualize roots:** Imagine roots growing from the soles of your feet into the ground, anchoring you firmly and providing stability.

**Tip:** Use this exercise whenever you feel overwhelmed or disconnected to bring yourself back to the present moment.

### Day 1: Johari Window - Understanding Known and Unknown Aspects of Yourself
**Exercise:** Deep Breathing Exercise

**Explanation:**
The Johari Window helps you understand your self-awareness and the perceptions of others by dividing your awareness into four quadrants: open, hidden, blind, and unknown.

**Relation to the Exercise:**
- **Understanding Boundaries:** Use deep breathing to calm your mind and reflect on the different aspects of yourself. Consider what is known to you and others (open), what you keep private (hidden), what others see but you don’t (blind), and what is unknown to both (unknown).
- **Self-Reflection:** This model encourages introspection, which pairs well with deep breathing to foster a deeper understanding of your boundaries.

### Day 2: The Circle of Control - Understanding What You Can and Cannot Control
**Exercise:** Grounding Exercise

**Explanation:**
The Circle of Control helps differentiate between what you can control (inner circle), what you can influence (middle circle), and what is outside your control (outer circle).

**Relation to the Exercise:**
- **Identifying Boundaries:** Grounding helps anchor you in the present, making it easier to identify areas of control and influence. Reflect on what aspects of your boundaries fall within these circles.
- **Reducing Anxiety:** By focusing on what you can control, grounding exercises help reduce stress about uncontrollable aspects, reinforcing the importance of well-defined boundaries.

### Day 3: The Pareto Principle - Focusing on the 20% of Efforts that Bring 80% of Results
**Exercise:** Body Scan Meditation

**Explanation:**
The Pareto Principle, or the 80/20 rule, states that 20% of efforts often produce 80% of results. It emphasizes prioritization.

**Relation to the Exercise:**
- **Assessing Boundaries:** Use body scan meditation to identify and focus on the key areas (the 20%) where boundaries are most needed and impactful.
- **Efficiency in Boundary Setting:** This principle helps you prioritize the most effective boundaries, aligning with the meditation’s goal of increasing body awareness and relaxation.

### Day 4: Nonviolent Communication (NVC) - Observing, Feeling, Needing, and Requesting Without Judgment
**Exercise:** Deep Breathing Exercise

**Explanation:**
NVC focuses on empathetic communication by observing without judgment, expressing feelings, identifying needs, and making requests.

**Relation to the Exercise:**
- **Setting Boundaries:** Deep breathing calms your mind, preparing you to use NVC to set boundaries. Practice observing your feelings, needs, and making clear, compassionate requests.
- **Effective Communication:** NVC enhances your ability to communicate boundaries clearly and non-judgmentally, supported by the clarity gained from deep breathing.

### Day 5: The Accountability Loop - Setting, Tracking, and Reviewing Your Boundaries Regularly
**Exercise:** Grounding Exercise

**Explanation:**
The Accountability Loop involves setting goals, tracking progress, and reviewing outcomes regularly to ensure accountability.

**Relation to the Exercise:**
- **Reinforcing Boundaries:** Grounding exercises help you stay present and mindful, aiding in regular tracking and reviewing of boundaries.
- **Consistency:** This model emphasizes the importance of consistently reinforcing and adjusting boundaries, supported by the stability provided by grounding exercises.
