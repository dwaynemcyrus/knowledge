- Digital book clubs are a social reading trend offering those involved the opportunity to discuss books, interact with authors, find new books, make new friends, etc.
- Examples of digital book clubs, such as [Bookclubs](https://bookclubs.com/) (formerly Bookclubsz), [BookClub by BookMovement](https://www.bookmovement.com/), and Fable
- Book clubs are fundamentally about connection. Connecting with members in a human way through books is the priority
- The tech (websites/apps) are just tools not the end, it’s the means
- It offers recommended reading lists, or “Folios,” created by authors and “curators” such as [LeVar Burton](https://fable.co/folio/levar-burton-book-recommendations-by-levar-burton), [David Sedaris](https://fable.co/folio/iconic-alcoholics-by-david-sedaris), [Anthony Doerr](https://fable.co/folio/anthony-doerrs-recommendations-by-anthony-doerr), [Malinda Lo](https://fable.co/folio/malinda-los-reading-list-by-malinda-lo), [Adam Grant](https://fable.co/folio/creativity-in-crisis-by-adam-grant), and [Jasmine Guillory](https://fable.co/folio/love-of-all-kinds-by-jasmine-guillory).
