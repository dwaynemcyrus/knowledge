### Overview of Core Steps to Setting Boundaries as a Trauma Survivor

#### Step 1: Self-Reflection and Awareness
- **Identify Core Issues**: Recognize the specific triggers and patterns related to trauma.
- **Understand Personal Needs**: Determine what makes you feel safe, respected, and valued.

#### Step 2: Define Boundaries
- **Clarify Limits**: Clearly articulate what is acceptable and what is not in terms of behavior from others.
- **Prioritize Boundaries**: Focus on the most critical boundaries first, especially those directly related to trauma triggers.

#### Step 3: Communication
- **Assertive Communication**: Learn and practice stating your boundaries clearly and assertively without aggression.
- **Use "I" Statements**: Frame your boundaries in terms of your own needs and feelings (e.g., "I feel uncomfortable when...").

#### Step 4: Implement Boundaries
- **Start Small**: Begin with less challenging situations to build confidence.
- **Consistency**: Be consistent in enforcing your boundaries to establish new patterns.

#### Step 5: Manage Reactions
- **Expect Pushback**: Understand that some people may react negatively to your boundaries.
- **Stay Firm**: Remain firm and calm in your stance, reiterating your boundaries if necessary.

#### Step 6: Seek Support
- **Therapy and Counseling**: Work with a therapist to navigate complex emotions and develop strategies.
- **Support Networks**: Engage with support groups or trusted friends who understand your journey.

#### Step 7: Self-Care and Reassessment
- **Regular Self-Care**: Prioritize activities that nurture your well-being and resilience.
- **Ongoing Reassessment**: Periodically review and adjust your boundaries as needed based on new experiences and growth.
