### Research
- ‚ÄúIf I only had an hour to chop on the tree, I would spend the first 45 minutes sharpening my axe.‚Äù - Abraham Lincoln
- Being able to research allows you to work with any industry, it‚Äôs fundamental that you become proficient at this.
#### The mark of a professional copywriter
- Your ability to research gives your writing potency and sets you apart from the others, this is like running, it's core to the craft ya know.
- **Writing without research is pointless, similar to design without research, it is not enough effective, it‚Äôs empty**
- Your research is your ammunition 
- Professionals research then win, they seek to understand the avatar, the problem, and the product. You want as much clarity on the current to dream state process as possible.
#### Target Market
1.  Sit with the client and ask "who is your best customer the one you would call 1000 times?" 
2.  If not that then the top types of customers of the client.
3.  If not that then you reverse engineer from the knowledge you have to identify your target.
#### Avatar
- Go out and find information about these people
- Talk to them in the real world, see how they are online, etc
#### Current State
- Questions to add
	- What do they fear?
	- What do they lie awake at night worrying about?
	- What words do they use to describe their pains and frustrations? This is very important! 
	- How are they perceived: self, work, family, strangers on the street 
- As a copywriter you want to be in the mind of the reader, write from what and how they speak to themselves because it convinces them that they are understood 
- As a marketer, we don‚Äôt convince we just find what they are already unhappy with and work from there
#### Dream state 
- Question to add
	- How would they feel about themselves if they made the change?
	- What enjoyable new experiences would they have?
	- How would others perceive them now in a positive light? Connecting with this is powerful.
	- What words would they use to describe their dream outcome? Amplify this because you cannot add anything to their head, so you must understand them and work with what they already have.
#### Roadblocks
- Questions to add
	- What‚Äôs keeping them from making their dream state today?
	- What mistakes are they making that are keeping them from getting what they want in their life?
	- What part of the obstacle does the avatar not understand or know about?
	- What is the one key roadblock that was fixed will allow them to move forward toward their dream outcome?
- Pay attention to what they are not talking about
- This takes some time with advanced critical thinking and problem solving
- If you can‚Äôt reveal the key issue you will not be able to position your writing
- You need to be as detailed, accurate, and clear as possible without it you are and will fail
#### Solution 
- Questions to add
	- What does the avatar need to do to overcome the key roadblock?
	- ‚ÄúIf they `insert solution`, then they will be able to `insert dream outcome`‚Äú
- They (the reader) usually have no idea what this (the solution) is
- Once this is crystal clear move on
#### Product
- Questions to add
	- How does the product to help the avatar implement this solution?
	- How does the product to help the reader increase the chances of success?
	- How does the product help the reader get the results faster?
	- How does the product help the reader get the results with less effort or sacrifice?
	- What makes the product fun?
	- What does your target market like about related products? Go out and find products that solve a similar problem to learn about them
- You need to understand how the avatar implements the solution, this is where you position the product to be fast, easy, etc FOR THEM
- The avatar may know what they need to do but don‚Äôt have the tool to accomplish the task
- Research existing solutions and learn what the avatar hates about them and then use that info in your copy to flip them
#### Research Sources
1. Clients and their customers, just ask them, with their online testimonials, etc. The client most likely has all of this information about their customers cause they‚Äôve been dealing with them.
2. Social media watch their YouTube videos, read their comments, look for any oversharing, and any other online presence they have
3. Amazon.com reviews look for books addressing a similar roadblock to yours with the five stars which tell you all the good and read the one stars which tells you all the bad
4. Reddit look for community subreddits and collect their language and their experiences it‚Äôs a great place to find the information about how people feel and what has worked well (and a lot) in the past
5. **Quora:** Find the questions and answers surrounding the problem your product solves
6. **Google:** There‚Äôs just a lot of information here, you‚Äôll be surprised

## Weaponizing Attention and Curiosity
He who controls the Dopamine, controls the Universe
- How to grab their attention and direct them
- The first battle you have to win is their attention in the communication game
- So how do we stand out in this battlefield of attention?
	- You need to break the pattern, find a way to be different from the environment 
	- **You want to present one of two things Opportunity (Something they want) or Threat (Something they fear) whichever is most interesting to break the pattern**
	- Whichever you choose there needs to be a pot of dopamine at the end of it to hit the pleasure neurotransmitters. This is released when there‚Äôs something cool happening, but also when you are chasing something good. Example: scrolling by social media for the one post that stands out! When they find it, the brain gives a hit of dopamine which is an activity experience. Similar to back in the day when hunters were out searching for prey and would find it
	- You want to tease them into seeking out the rest of the dopamine hit, 
	- Whenever you present something to catch their attention, they need to know there‚Äôs a reward on the other side (dopamine), teasing that there is something valuable or dangerous or relevant to them down the line with your subject line and you need to tap into that
	- **Dopamine is what makes the world go around, we live in an attention based society, he who gives people dopamine in the right way, you have them doing whatever you want.** 
	- If you can dangle dopamine and have them chase some sort of future payoff (the reward and hit) you win
	- Adding curiosity, mystery, and intrigue are like dumping gasoline on the fire and if you can add that to your work you got them
### How do you engineer curiosity?

- Have curiosity, mystery, and intrigue in everything that you write, within each step you want them to get a hit, it‚Äôs how you lead people.

You want the reader to think this is not what I thought it would be so let me find out what it is.

## How to Write Fascinations
### The ‚ÄúLego Blocks‚Äù of Copywriting 
- Before you can dive into writing emails landing pages and Facebook ads we need to learn about the smallest of building blocks as a copywriter, fascinations
- If you learn to write killer fascinations you have no problem capturing your readers attention, which is the first and hardest battle
- Mastering the art of fascinations literally will make the reader addicted to you
- What is a Fascination? F**ascination is a short line or two that combines desire with intrigue to create a potent effect in the mind of the reader.** And written well makes the reader rabbid to read the rest of your work and take action at the end. **You want to combine the thing they care about with intrigue, a specific tease, with a curiosity element is how you do it right. It creates a splinter in their mind.**
- Examples to get a feel of what this is like:
	- Headline the teachers favorite, ‚Äú wife of famous movie star swears under oath her new perfume does not contain an illegal sexual stimulant!‚Äù, there‚Äôs drama, specificity, intrigue, we need to now know what this perfume is.
	- ‚ÄúMy weird hashtag strategy that helped me grow by 52,000 followers in one month!‚Äù, another headline. Specific details that one would care a lot about along with some mystery.
	- ‚ÄúAmazing secret discovered by one legged golfer adds 50 yards to your drives, eliminates hooks and slices‚Ä¶ and can slash up to 10 strokes from your game almost overnight!‚Äù, another headline. It‚Äôs interesting and intrigues it just cannot be ignored by golfers. 
- Essentially all fascinations have two main elements
	1. An implied opportunity or threat
	2. Specific details that make a reader think that there‚Äôs something real on the other side, with enough information to create an information gap. If you are the Avatar you need to be compelled to solve the **fascination**.
- After research you will do a minimum of 100 fascinations as this is your ammunition 
- As you are out in the world, copy and document fascinations that you see in use
- **When you control curiosity you control attention and in turn control people, get great at fascinations**

Example: Are you afraid you'll get stuck with a depressing "white picket fence" life if you don't make real money NOW?

#### Specific question

Example: If you are tired of falling flat on your face when talking with that beautiful girl, then you need to learn the 7 questions that will get any girl wildly interested in you.

"When" relates to times and is not used as often
#### Quickest, Easiest
Example: *The quickest way to boost your confidence and get that millionaire "glow"*
"Quickest, Easiest" implies novelty and superiority, these adjectives amplify curiosity
#### Truth
Example: *The truth about trading crypto that will ensure you're on the right side of history*
"Truth" implies information gap to avoid pain and or solve problem, a powerful formula
#### Better than
Example: *Better than caffeine. Discover the best (legal) supplement for greater focus and discipline*
"Better than" solution based over product and sets the readers expectations
#### Single
Example: The single step you must take before all of your sales calls to maintain frame and close like the Wolf of Wall Street
"Single" adds importance to the topic, magnifies the importance, makes the information gap they have seem smaller and compels them to want to solve it, and take action

## DIC Framework
(Disrupt Intrigue Click)
High curiosity short form copy
- This framework is a series of steps and a foundation, it‚Äôs all about grabbing attention and getting them to click
- Steps: Disrupt > Intrigue > Click
- Disrupt: This is where you catch their attention, if there‚Äôs an image use a disrupting image because they will see this first, it needs to be different from their environment. Think pattern interrupt. It's also the first few lines of long-form copy. Instill some intrigue and curiosity, little bits.
- Intrigue: Amplify and stack the curiosity and intrigue until the reader can‚Äôt help but take action. Multiple fascinations, not-statements, creating unanswered questions, etc use all the tools to tease. When they get to the end they need to have low-grade anxiety, the splinter, for them to desire an answer. 
- Click: Simplistic is a single line with a CTA (call-to-action), now you simply direct them to take the action *‚Äúhey, you want the answer click here‚Äù*. A good CTA contains all 3 elements of the DIC, as most people will scan the email to the bottom. Be sure your CTA doesn‚Äôt reveal the *‚Äúanswer‚Äù* to the secret you have been teasing, you want to keep the mystery alive.
- A Bonus can be a PS at the end of the email. For example, to drop even more intrigue on the reader or to amplify their pain or desire. 
- **DIC works well with cold traffic, people who don‚Äôt know anything about your brand and products**
- You can use this to get people to consume more content videos, blogs, podcasts, etc. 
- Recommends most short-form copy stay under 250 words
- The primary text is the intrigue
- Emojis are cool for disrupts in subject lines
- On IG the CTA is usually referenced to the link in the bio
- DIC is a format you will use A LOT as a copywriter
## PAS Framework
(Pain/Desire Amplify Solution)
Get them to move from the short-form copy...
- This framework is great for inspiring action and providing motivation especially once they have already discovered the answer. This magnifies the emotion to take action.
- Steps: pain/desire > amplify > solution
- Pain/Desire: You can use either one, dangle the carrot or whip with the stick. This is where you stop them for their attention and hit them with a great emotional experience by amplifying their greatest button pain or desire
- Amplify: Humans have many ways of self-soothing and minimizing the emotional intensity of our pain and desire because it‚Äôs easier than changing to fulfil the desire or fix the pain. 
	- To get them to take action you must stoke the flames of their existing pain or desire until it propels them to action
	- You do this with sensory experience you want them to play a movie in their mind to trigger a corresponding emotion. Sight smell taste sound and touch, you don‚Äôt have to use all, but the more you can the better and more vivid the movie. Use this sensory information that‚Äôs around that pain or desire
	- You do this with future pacing, you want to give a taste of what could happen if they don‚Äôt solve the problem by highlighting the reality of not taking the action. However, it‚Äôs better to go the positive route than the negative in most situations.
	- Show them how their pain or desire can connect to their higher need think Maslows you can amplify the compulsion to act.
	- You can show them how their situation or dream affects their status in the eyes of others to magnify the emotion
	- Take their desire and show them how it connects to their greatest wants and needs
- Solution: Direct them with a simple line to direct their action to resolve their heightened state of emotion. There are a few levers you can pull to enact this:
	- A reader believes there‚Äôs a limited time to take advantage of an opportunity, the pain/desire is amplified, you want to create urgency and play with time 
	- Scarcity, this is more about the availability and or quantity of the solution to solve the problem. A boat with 2 seats remaining with 20 people in line.
	- You can one last time crank on the dial of their emotions to push them over the edge.
- PSA is how you get people who have already experienced DIC to take another or the initial value exchange.
## HSO Framework
(Hook Story Offer)
Shift their brains with the power of story 
- This is all about shifting belief and inspiring desire and curiosity using the power of story
- 3 main sections: hook > story > offer
- Hook: Catching the attention and getting them to want to read the story, usually done with one strong fascination (a bribe to read)
- Story: Humans have evolved to change beliefs, make decisions, learn and absorb information through the power of story. Think of the hieroglyphs, books of scriptures, because stories guide people, shift beliefs and allow the reader to sympathetically experience the same emotions and transformations as the characters inside. Similar to when you are watching a movie and cry or feel an emotion. 
- All stories follow the hero‚Äôs journey
- The journey starts with describing the known world, then the call to adventure
- Important storytelling tips
	- Start the story at the height of drama at the moment when all seems to be lost, preview the drama, the scary, the wild. Think Bond movie trailers 
	- Ensure the main character means something to the reader, and some aspirational elements so the reader can identify with the character and see themselves as the character to become emotionally involved. The main character and the avatar need to match. We are able to relate to people who are like us. 
	- Skip parts of the story to create intrigue. Lead the reader up to the point of despair then fast forward to the point when they are living the dream. This creates an extreme information gap which creates curiosity that you can capitalize on in the next phase. 
	- Offer: Direct them to take action to calm their curiosity to fill in the information gap or direct them to follow the path of the character you have walked them through (the journey) so they can mimic it themselves. The CTA is similar to the others, DIC and PSA. 
	- **HSO is great way to get readers to experience the solution.**
## Short Form Copy Best Practices
Avoid the land mines and win
- Most short-form copy should be under 150 (250 max) words to force you to only use impactful words to keep the reader's attention and avoid mistakes, per email, FB ad, etc plus it‚Äôs about the most a read will likely take the time to consume.
- Mini skirt rule, Long enough to cover the important parts, but short enough to keep things interesting. 
- In copy, reviews ask, *"Does this add to the copy? Does this take away from the copy? Is this neutral?* This exercise keeps you in line with only what adds to the experience and end goal.
- Each piece of short-form copy should have only one idea or you begin to sound crazy and dilute your power and effectiveness. You want to bring all of your persuasive power to bare.
- The **thread**, your copy, should all comment one into another. Each line should make you want to read the next line. There are no gaps or friction, each line builds on the next. It needs to be fun and exciting you are competing with everything that can distract them, their dog, TikTok, IG, etc.
- Use normal fonts and keep the grammar and spelling on point, don‚Äôt use words like *"this"*, or *"it"*, be specific, always.
- Avoid long paragraphs you want to maintain 1-2 lines because on mobile it can turn to 8 which is uncomfortable for readers. Keep it to one complete idea in a paragraph, never split up an idea. 
- Use bold, italic, and underlines sparingly these are meant for emphasis and to draw attention. Too many and it loses the effect. Once or twice per short form copy.
- Emojis when appropriate, they add some emotional context, they were made to express emotional subtext.
- Avoid sales clich√©s, readers are highly in tune with these and trigger sales guards and they dismiss you. Keep it personal you are speaking to one person.
- Avoid overly formal language, make it feel like a conversation between two friends, make it personal communication, and remember whoever can show they care the most connects the most and wins. Have fun with your writing as the reader will pick up on this! And enjoy it too.
- Writing can be fun make it fun and make it fun for the reader
