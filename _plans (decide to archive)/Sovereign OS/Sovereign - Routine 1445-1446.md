# Mindset Routine
## Evening Thinking
Thinking with your blackboard to allow your mind to be free to express itself for at the minimum of 20 minutes.
## Evening Reflections
### Assess
The aim is to collect of your notes and random pieces of information from the day to process.
1. Check your habits in your daily log to ensure they are checked off.
2. Look over your current projects and task for any completions to checkoff, updates to make, and next physical actions to be set.
3. Check your second brain and slip box for any open ends that need to be closed or next physical actions to be set
4. Check your browsers (desktop & mobile) for tabs to be closed or saved in a group for tomorrow, or next physical actions to be set.
5. Process any open apps on your devices and make any notes for the next physical actions to take.
6. Check your scratchpads and inboxes (digital and physical) for information to be processed quickly, the 2min stuff. (Notes, Journal, Sketchbook, Things, GoodNotes)
7. Take note and archive any kind words or acts done for you today.
8. Log your money, what's coming in (revenue), what's going out (expense), to where, and how it was taken from you.
9. Log any of your sins and good deeds for the day.
10. Log any wins, fails, and learns of the day.

### Debrief
1. Review and fill out the content template in today's daily log.
2. Answer your [[Reflection Questions|evening reflection questions]] to get a sense of how your day has gone in your daily log.
3. Write a mini-story about the day that you could tell others about.
4. Vlog your reflections about the day similar to the move [[Avatar]].
5. Reflect on the day with some additional thoughts and commentary in your diary.

### Prepare
1. Review the Calendar 3 days back and 3 ahead for actions to note.
2. Read through this weeks review document to check that you are on the right path. 
3. Make updates to your projects and actions so that you are clear for tomorrow.
4. Check that you have a piece of content, knowledge, and artwork that's actively being worked on.
5. Fill out tomorrow's actions in your daily log.
6. Think about what creative expression you need to attempt tomorrow for a few minutes and sketch/write some ideas as they come to you
