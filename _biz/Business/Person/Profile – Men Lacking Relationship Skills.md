## Norms to Change
[[Enemy to Fight|New ways of being]] to get Samira to see and desire the value in for her life.

For men who have been hurt and are seeking to improve their emotional intelligence (EQ) and communication skills, certain accepted norms or beliefs may need to change to benefit their wellbeing and transformation. Here’s a list of norms that could be addressed to facilitate their growth and development:

1. Suppressing Emotions
	- Old Norm: Believing that showing emotions is a sign of weakness.
	- New Norm: Understanding that expressing emotions is a strength and crucial for emotional health.
2. Stoicism as Strength
	- Old Norm: Valuing stoicism and emotional suppression as markers of strength.
	- New Norm: Recognizing that vulnerability and emotional openness are important aspects of true strength.
3. Communication as Confrontation
	- Old Norm: Viewing communication about feelings and needs as potential conflict.
	- New Norm: Seeing communication as a tool for connection and understanding.
4. Fear of Judgment
	- Old Norm: Worrying that expressing feelings will lead to judgment or ridicule.
	- New Norm: Trusting that sharing emotions can lead to support and deeper connections.
5. Avoiding Vulnerability
	- Old Norm: Avoiding vulnerability to protect themselves from being hurt.
	- New Norm: Embracing vulnerability as a path to authentic relationships and personal growth.
6. Self-Reliance to a Fault
	- Old Norm: Believing that they should handle all problems on their own.
	- New Norm: Recognizing the value of seeking help and support from others.
7. Misunderstanding Masculinity
	- Old Norm: Equating masculinity with emotional detachment and control.
	- New Norm: Redefining masculinity to include emotional awareness and empathy.
8. Reactive Behavior
	- Old Norm: Responding to emotional situations with anger or withdrawal.
	- New Norm: Learning to respond thoughtfully and calmly to emotional triggers.
9. Neglecting Self-Care
	- Old Norm: Prioritizing work and external responsibilities over personal wellbeing.
	- New Norm: Valuing self-care and recognizing its importance for overall health and effectiveness.
10.	Fear of Intimacy
	- Old Norm: Keeping emotional distance to avoid getting hurt.
	- New Norm: Allowing themselves to be emotionally intimate and trusting in close relationships.
11.	Communication as Weakness
	- Old Norm: Believing that discussing feelings and needs is unmanly.
	- New Norm: Understanding that effective communication is key to healthy relationships.
12.	Ignoring Mental Health
	- Old Norm: Believing that mental health issues should be kept private and dealt with alone.
	- New Norm: Acknowledging the importance of mental health and seeking professional support when needed.
13.	Relationship Misconceptions
	- Old Norm: Thinking that relationships should be easy and not require effort.
	- New Norm: Realizing that healthy relationships require ongoing effort and communication.
14.	Conflict Avoidance
	- Old Norm: Avoiding difficult conversations to prevent conflict.
	- New Norm: Embracing constructive conflict as a way to resolve issues and strengthen relationships.
15.	Perfectionism
	- Old Norm: Striving for perfection and feeling inadequate when falling short.
	- New Norm: Accepting imperfections and focusing on continuous growth and improvement.
16.	Emotional Literacy
	- Old Norm: Having a limited emotional vocabulary and understanding.
	- New Norm: Expanding emotional literacy and becoming more aware of their own and others’ emotions.
17.	Overemphasis on Logic
	- Old Norm: Prioritizing logic and dismissing emotions as irrational.
	- New Norm: Balancing logic with emotional insight to make well-rounded decisions.
18.	Fear of Appearing Weak
	- Old Norm: Avoiding any behavior that might be perceived as weak or vulnerable.
	- New Norm: Embracing vulnerability as a key component of resilience and strength.

By addressing and transforming these norms, hurt men can improve their emotional intelligence, enhance their communication skills, and build healthier, more fulfilling relationships.
